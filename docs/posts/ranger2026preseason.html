Ranger Suárez

The Red Sox made a slough of additions in the off season coming off of their first postseason appearance since 2021. The most notable of which was the signing of Ranger Suárez, who came over from the Phillies on a 5-year, $130 million deal. He was a part of the top rotation in baseball last year, paired with Cristopher Sanchez and Zack Wheeler, will he be able to move the needle in Boston’s rotation to become the new best rotation in baseball?

In his time with the Phillies Suarez has been recognized around the league as a sinker-ball pitcher, in other words, a pitcher looking to create weak contact rather than blow hitters away with strikeout stuff. His sinker hangs around average in measures of pitch shape and batted ball outcomes, but his changeup and cutter set him apart once he establishes the sinker.
